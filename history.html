  • Barabashov N. P.

    Studies of the Moon and other planets of the Solar System at the Kharkov Observatory (which later became the Institute of Astronomy) were started by Acad. Fessenkov V.G. in the 1930s.
    The founder of the world wide-known Kharkov school of planetary science, which gave a number of famous scientists and made a number of world famous discoveries and studies, was Acad. Barabashov N.P.
    Information about the Moon's surface, Martian atmosphere, studies of the photometric properties of atmospheres of Venus, Jupiter, Saturn obtained by the researchers of Kharkov Observatory in the 1930-60`s ensured greater efficiency in the first planetary space program planning. Astronomers of Kharkov National University took part in the preparation and reduction of the results of almost all space born researches of the Moon, Mars, Venus and Halley Comet carried out in the USSR.

  • Akimov L. A.

    Many outstanding astronomers were students and successors of Acad. Barabashov's scientific research. Among them was Professor Leonid Afanasievich Akimov, who later formed a group of employees that became the Department of Physics of the Sun, Moon and Planets. As an independent unit, the Department of Physics of the Sun, Moon and Planets was separated in 1993 during the reorganization of the Department of "Astrophysics" and is the successor to the classical direction of astrophysical research of the Sun, Moon and planets, laid down by Acad. Barabashov N.P. Research objects: the sun, moon, planets of the solar system. Research methods: photometric, polarimetric and spectrographic observations, as well as theoretical research.
    One of Akimov's fundamental discoveries was the disk function (named after - Akimov's formula), which describes the distribution of brightness over the lunar disk, has a rather simple and concise form and gives a more accurate distribution, unlike the classical functions of Lommel-Zeeliger, Minnaert, McEwen, Hapke. Akimov's formula is still used today.

  • Shkuratov Yu. G.

    Another successor and student of Acad. Barabashov N.P. is Yuri Grigorievich Shkuratov, who, together with his students and colleagues, created group that was later transformed into the Department of Remote Sensing of Planets in 1993. The department worked in two directions: 1) processing of data from space and ground observations of the Moon and planets; 2) laboratory and theoretical modeling of light scattering by surfaces of complex structure.
    Some of the well-known results of Shkuratov's research are: the method of phase relations, a new photometric function derived from the concept of a fractal-like surface structure, and a number of studies of the photometric, polarimetric and spectral properties of the lunar regolith

  • Kaydash V. G.

    The Lunar-Planetary Group arose in the 2010s in the process of joint work and merger of the departments of methods of remote sensing of planets and the physics of the Sun, Moon and planets.
    The head of the lunar-planetary group in 2021 is Kaydash Vadim Grigorievich, and the ideological inspirers and founders of Shkuratov Yuri Grigorievi and Korokhin Viktor Valentinovich.

    Korokhin V. V.

    The main areas of research of the Lunar-planetary group are:
    1. Investigation of the photometric function of the lunar surface.
    2. Development of methods for remote sensing of the lunar surface and other surface Solar system bodies.
    3. Remote sensing of the planetary surfaces.
